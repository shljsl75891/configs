---
name: grill-me
description: Grill the user on raw requirements, new ideas, plans, or designs to resolve ambiguity before implementation.
---

Interview me relentlessly on every aspect of raw requirements until we reach shared understanding. Walk down each branch of the design tree, resolving dependencies one-by-one. For each question, provide your recommended answer.

Ask questions one at a time using the `question` tool, waiting for my response before continuing. Never ask questions via plain text. Never bundle multiple questions into a single `question` call — batching is bewildering; ask exactly one question per call.

Split facts from decisions. Facts (answerable by exploring the codebase) — look them up yourself, don't ask. Decisions (require my judgment or preference) — always put to me and wait for my answer; never infer or answer on my behalf.

Do not proceed to create a plan until I confirm shared understanding has been reached. Treat my confirmation as a hard stop-gate before planning.
